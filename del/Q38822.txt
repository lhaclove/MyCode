CC   -!- FUNCTION: Aux/IAA proteins are short-lived transcriptional factors
CC       that function as repressors of early auxin response genes at low
CC       auxin concentrations. Repression is thought to result from the
CC       interaction with auxin response factors (ARFs), proteins that bind
CC       to the auxin-responsive promoter element (AuxRE). Plays a central
CC       role in auxin regulation of root growth, in gravitropism, and in
CC       lateral root formation (PubMed:9895319). Regulated by an auxin-
CC       induced protein turnover (PubMed:14617065). Formation of
CC       heterodimers with ARF proteins may alter their ability to modulate
CC       early auxin response genes expression. When activated by
CC       cytokinin, restricts the expression of the PIN genes to the
CC       vascular transition zone (PubMed:19039136). Induction of SHY2 in
CC       the vascular transition zone restricts BRX expression to down-
CC       regulate PIN3 and thus limit meristem growth, but proper SHY2
CC       expression requires BRX (PubMed:21149702). Involved in meristem
CC       growth and in determining its size (PubMed:20605455). May
CC       participate in strigolactone signaling to regulate meristem size
CC       and lateral root formation (PubMed:23425316).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11884676, ECO:0000269|PubMed:12036262,

CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:23425316, ECO:0000269|PubMed:9895319}.

CC   -!- DOMAIN: The N-terminal half of the protein contains two conserved
CC       domains I and II. Domain I includes a slightly degenerated ERF-
CC       associated amphiphilic repression (EAR) motif which seems to be
CC       involved in the activity of transcriptional repression. Domain II
CC       is required for the correct degradation of the protein through the
CC       SCF-mediated ubiquitin-proteasome pathway. Interactions between
CC       Aux/IAA proteins and auxin response factors (ARFs) occur through
CC       their C-terminal dimerization domains III and IV.
CC   -!- PTM: Phosphorylated by phytochrome A in vitro.
