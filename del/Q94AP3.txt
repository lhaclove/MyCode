CC   -!- FUNCTION: Required for secondary wall formation in fibers,
CC       especially in short days conditions. Promotes indole metabolism
CC       and transport (e.g. tryptophan, neoglucobrassicin and auxin
CC       (indole-3-acetic acid)). May prevent salicylic-acid (SA)
CC       accumulation. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:20497379,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:20935503, ECO:0000269|PubMed:22978675}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Vacuole membrane
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:17151019, ECO:0000269|PubMed:20497379}; Multi-
CC       pass membrane protein {ECO:0000269|PubMed:17151019,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:20497379}. Note=Also detected in transvacuolar
CC       strands.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Mostly expressed in stems and hypocotyls, also
CC       present in seedlings, root, leaves, flowers and siliques.
CC       Ubiquitous, mostly expressed in vascular tissues and secondary
CC       wall-forming cells, including developing xylem vessels and fibers.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:20497379}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: In stems, defect in cell elongation
CC       resulting in dwarf and bushy plants with altered mechanical
CC       properties, as well as little to no secondary cell walls in
CC       fibers, including xylary and interfascicular fibers; these
CC       symptoms are partly reversed by continuous light conditions. At
CC       the flowering stage, red, dry and bent downwards stem apices.
CC       General repression of indole metabolism, including tryptophan,
CC       neoglucobrassicin and auxin (indole-3-acetic acid). Broad-spectrum
CC       resistance to vascular pathogens, including the bacteria Ralstonia
CC       solanacearum and Xanthomonas campestris pv. campestris, and the
CC       fungi Verticillium dahliae and Verticillium albo-atrum in a
CC       salicylic-acid- (SA-) dependent manner. SA accumulation in roots.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:20497379, ECO:0000269|PubMed:20935503,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:22978675}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the drug/metabolite transporter (DMT)
CC       superfamily. Plant drug/metabolite exporter (P-DME) (TC 2.A.7.4)
CC       family. {ECO:0000305}.
